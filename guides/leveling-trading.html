Leveling Trading

The Trading skill governs your discount when buying from merchants and your bonus when selling to them. It's one of nine player skills (data/configs/achievements/max_level_trading.cfg) and the easiest skill to level without combat.

How XP is earned

Every completed buy or sell transaction at a merchant grants Trading XP. The value of the transaction is what feeds the bar — flipping one expensive item is roughly equivalent to many cheap ones, so volume or value works.

The outpost perk that compounds with skill

One of only three perks in data/configs/outpostperks/ is trade-related:

Build / level the trade-discount perk at your home outpost as soon as you have a stable income. The discount stacks with whatever skill-based reduction Trading itself grants.

Strategy

  1. Mine and smelt, then sell ingots. The biggest reliable trading-XP source early. Tier-1 ingots have small price tags but you'll have thousands by the time you're advanced.
  2. Sell at the right outpost. Outpost specialisations affect which items have high demand. A mining-themed outpost pays more for ingots; a tech outpost for electronics; a farming outpost for fruit / seeds / saplings.
  3. Buy vendor-supplied items below their crafting cost. Motherboards, mirrors, and similar electronic parts are usually cheaper to buy in bulk than to chase as drops. Roll a trade-discount perk and stock up.
  4. Loot then sell. Cleared a bunker or pirate ship? Bring everything you don't need to the nearest outpost. Even creature resources and low-tier components add up.
  5. Don't carry corruption-stained items into civilised outposts — some quest stages disable trading temporarily, but otherwise stains are just an inventory penalty.
