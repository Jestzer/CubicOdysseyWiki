Leveling Trading

The Trading skill governs your discount when buying and your bonus when selling. It's one of nine player skills (data/configs/achievements/max_level_trading.cfg) and the easiest skill to level without combat.

Set your level to highlight the right stage below.

What to do at your level

Beginning level 1–9
Convert mining surplus into qubits.
  • Smelt before you sell. Raw ore is worth less than the ingot. Run a furnace before the first merchant trip.
  • Visit the nearest outpost and dump everything non-essential: low-tier ingots, mob drops, captured creature resources. Volume builds early XP fast.
  • Start unlocking the Trade Discount outpost perk as soon as you control an outpost. Every level shaves 2% off merchant prices.
  • Don't worry about base_demand yet — at this stage you're selling whatever you have, not flipping.
Mid game level 10–19
Buy low (vendor stock), use; sell high (high-demand mob/loot items).
  • Push Trade Discount at your outpost. The perk applies before the skill discount, so they stack.
  • Buy vendor-supplied parts in bulk — capacitors, mirrors, wiring, even motherboards if you can afford them. Their cost at merchants is below their crafting / scavenge cost; see the bottom table.
  • Start triaging loot: keep high-demand items (the top of the flip table); sell everything else.
  • Avoid burning trade volume on Glowing / endgame ores yet — they fetch much more once you've levelled.
Advanced level 20–29
Multi-stop trade loops and outpost specialisation.
  • Outpost specialisations affect which items have inflated demand. A mining outpost pays more for ingots; a tech outpost for electronics; a farming outpost for fruits / seeds / saplings.
  • Plan multi-stop loops: mine on planet A → smelt → sell ingots at mining outpost → buy motherboards / parts at tech outpost → return.
  • Quest rewards from faction merchants stack with Trading XP from the sale completion. Run faction quests that pay in items, not qubits.
  • Start crafting tier-3 / tier-4 items to flip, not just use — see the high-value flip table.
Endgame level 30+
High-value flips, max perk, large volume per stop.
  • Max the Trade Discount perk at every outpost you hold. The compounding discount makes bulk stockpiling cheap.
  • Flip Glowing ingots and tier-5 gem outputs through specialised merchants. The top of the high-demand table tells you where the qubits are.
  • Hoard Motherboards bought below price and use them for the few crafts in the Motherboards guide rather than reselling — vendor purchase < craft value.
  • Cash flow is no longer the bottleneck — bank everything toward MK3 ship components and Suit 4 / Scanner 4 / Jetpack 4.

How XP is earned

Every completed buy or sell transaction at a merchant grants Trading XP. The value of the transaction is what feeds the bar — flipping one expensive item is roughly equivalent to many cheap ones, so volume or value works.
