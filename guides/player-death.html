What happens when you die

The player character ships with 60 life (from data/configs/characters/player.cfg). That's a small bar — see the HP comparison below — and you will die. When you do, the game spawns a tombstone holding your inventory and reverts you to the nearest spawn anchor.

The tombstone (Death Chest)

On death the engine deploys dpl.deathchest at the spot you fell:

FieldValue
Identifierdpl.deathchest
DisplayDeath Chest
World modeldata/models/c_objects/tombstone.glm
InvincibleTrue (can't be destroyed by mobs or explosions while it exists)

Notes from the .cfg:

  • The model is literally tombstone.glm — Cubic Odyssey's tombstones are rendered as voxel grave markers. Easy to spot from the air.
  • It's flagged invincible TRUE so combat near your corpse won't accidentally erase your stuff.
  • It's isHidden TRUE in the catalog so you won't see it on Recipes / Encyclopedia listings — it's a system-spawned object.

Recovering your stuff

  1. Respawn — you reload at the nearest spawn anchor (your ship, a bed at your outpost, or the world's default spawn).
  2. Travel back — open the world map; tombstones show as a distinct icon on the planet you died on. If you died in space or another system, you'll need to fly there.
  3. Activate the tombstone — same interaction as any container. Empty its contents back into your inventory.
  4. The chest persists until you empty it; it doesn't decay on a timer in the configs. (Don't take that as a guarantee against logical despawn under heavy world load — recover sooner rather than later.)

Doctors (NPC interaction)

The npc_doctor.cfg character provides a DOCTOR interaction at outposts. Their stats:

Interaction typeDOCTOR
Life1000 (huge — they're effectively unkillable in normal combat)
Shield capacity10000 (extra invulnerability layer)
Weaponwep.rifle.sma15e.3

Doctors don't revive you (the death cycle has already happened) — they heal you back to full life after respawn and can cure status effects. Visit one before raiding a high-tier zone.

Friendly questgiver NPCs are deliberately invulnerable-tier (1000 HP); they're not balanced against you. Quest stages depend on them being present.

How to avoid dying

  • Wear a Suit. Every suit tier adds raw HP via its m_attributes. Suit 4 (the motherboard-gated craft) roughly doubles your effective life.
  • Carry shields and batteries. Batteries refill stamina, which feeds dodge and sprint — your real survivability is more about not getting hit than tanking hits.
  • Keep a Jetpack on the hotbar. Falls and surprise mob clusters are the most common causes of death after the first hour.
  • Save before risky raids. Cubic Odyssey's save system is fragile (autosaves can bury good manual saves; see the CubicOdysseyVault project for context). Don't rely on autosave alone.
  • Spawn a landing beacon (dpl.ship.landing.beacon) before going into a known-dangerous biome so you can quick-respawn nearby.
  • Don't engage a Zombie Boomer in melee. They have 160+ HP and explode on death.

What you don't lose

  • Skill XP — Mining, Trading, Crafting, etc. progression is preserved.
  • Recipes / blueprints — these are account-level (see 93_blueprints.sav).
  • Outpost perks & structures — your bases are unaffected.
  • Quest progress — quest state is in 93_quests.sav and survives death.
  • Ships you weren't piloting — only the ship you were in is affected; docked ships are safe.
