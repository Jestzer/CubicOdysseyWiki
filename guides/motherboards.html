Finding Motherboards Tier 3

res.motherboard

A tier-3 electronic component required by every tier-4 personal upgrade and every MK3 ship component. Motherboards are not craftable — the only way to get them is to find or buy them.

Set your Crafting level to highlight what to prioritise and grey out recipes you can't make yet.

What to spend motherboards on, at your level

Pre-craft Crafting 1–7
No motherboard recipes are unlocked yet. Bank them.
  • Every motherboard recipe in the game needs at least Crafting 8. Until you cross that threshold, motherboards are paperweights.
  • Push Crafting XP: craft anything tier-1 / tier-2 you can. Volume matters more than item choice at this stage.
  • If you're desperate for qubits, you can sell motherboards — but their base_price (30) is low and you'll regret it once Crafting 8 hits.
First unlocks Crafting 8–15
Most motherboard recipes open up at level 8 — pick the ones that compound.
  • Backpack 5 (8 motherboards) — biggest single quality-of-life jump, increases all mining and looting runs.
  • Mining Laser 4 (6 motherboards) — unlocks tier-5 gem mining, feeds Trading too.
  • Jetpack 4 (10 motherboards) — mobility.
  • Scanner 4 (12 motherboards) — expensive but enables tier-6 Matter hunting; defer until you have plenty in stock.
  • Skip tier-4 personal weapons for now if you only own one of each archetype — pick the one you actually use.
All recipes Crafting 16+
Every motherboard recipe is craftable. Aim spend at the largest gains.
  • MK3 ship components are the biggest sinks at 32–48 motherboards each. If you fly seriously, prioritise these.
  • If you don't fly much, all tier-4 personal weapons + Suit 4 are within reach (6–12 motherboards each).
  • Don't waste them on duplicate weapons across archetypes — pick the SMG / Rifle / Marksman you'll actually carry.
  • Stockpile a small reserve for another ship loadout — engine + shield + cargo costs add up quickly.

What the data tells us

  • No recipe produces a motherboard. A scan of all 900 crafting recipes in data/configs/recipes/ finds zero that output res.motherboard. The item must be obtained from world loot or merchants.
  • It does not appear in any creature / bunker / pirate randomset. A grep across data/configs/randomsets/*.cfg for res.motherboard returns no matches. Motherboards therefore drop from hand-placed loot rather than from random mob drops.
  • Negative base_demand (-11000). Items with strongly negative demand are flagged as vendor stock rather than player-sold goods — the engine biases merchants to supply them.
  • Tier 3 with recycle value 3 and base price 30. Stack size 100.

Best places to look

  1. Merchants at outposts and space stations. Most reliable. Engine flags motherboards as vendor stock (negative demand); higher-tier outposts carry more of them. The Trade Discount outpost perk reduces price.
  2. Abandoned bunkers — the underground multi-room bunkers (the engine uses data/scripts/bunker.lua to lay them out). Their generic randomsets (bunker_bedroom, bunker_computer_room, bunker_dining_room, bunker_gear, bunker_money, bunker_resources, bunker_underground_farm) seed sub-components (wiring, connector, mirror, capacitor); the bunker generator places hand-curated loot chests in computer / lab rooms with motherboards.
  3. Pirate ship wrecks & space-station ruins. Lab modules and electronics crates frequently contain motherboards.
  4. Salvaging destroyed enemy ships. Medium / large wrecks scanned with a Scanner usually yield motherboards.
  5. Quest rewards. Especially useful early when you don't have high-tier merchant access.
